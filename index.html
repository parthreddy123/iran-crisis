Historical Precedent

How Similar Crises Resolved

1990
Gulf War (Iraq-Kuwait) — 4.3M bbl/day disrupted. Oil spiked from $21 to $41 (+95%). Resolution: 7-month military campaign. Price normalized within 3 months of ceasefire. Lesson: military resolution restores supply quickly once achieved, but the timeline to get there is unpredictable.
2019
Abqaiq-Khurais attack — 5.7M bbl/day disrupted for <48 hours. Oil spiked 15% intraday, reversed within 2 weeks. Lesson: markets discount short disruptions rapidly. The question is always duration, not peak.
1979
Iranian Revolution — 3.4M bbl/day lost for 12+ months. Oil tripled from $14 to $40. Triggered global recession. Lesson: prolonged disruption from Iran specifically reshapes global energy architecture. Japan and South Korea built their SPRs in response.
2022
Russia-Ukraine war — No physical disruption but sanctions-driven rerouting. India pivoted to Russian crude within weeks, becoming the largest buyer. Lesson: India's rapid pivot capability exists and is now being stress-tested at scale.

Pattern: every major disruption lasting >3 months triggers permanent structural shifts in sourcing, storage, and policy. We are at Day 11.
